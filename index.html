Maintenance, Upkeep & Checklists

Maintenance Checklists

Weekly Checklist
  • Check moisture in wall pockets; they dry out faster.
  • Deadhead faded roses and perennials to encourage rebloom.
  • Inspect leaves for aphids or powdery mildew (common in Baramulla humidity).
  • Weed the slope bed to prevent root competition.
Monthly Checklist
  • Deep feed flowering shrubs with liquid potassium.
  • Edge the stone border around the slope bed.
  • Prune back any aggressive vines (Clematis/Ivy).
  • Apply organic mulch to maintain root coolness.
Quarterly / Yearly
  • Autumn: Plant bulbs (Saffron/Tulips) and dig up Dahlia tubers.
  • Winter: Heavy mulch base of Peonies and Hydrangeas before snow.
  • Spring: Hard prune Mock Orange and Butterfly Bush.
  • Yearly: Inspect tree health (Maple/Birch) for storm damage.

Climate Optimization

Dealing with Snowfall

In Baramulla, snow acts as an insulator. Avoid shaking snow off woody branches unless it threatens to snap them. For perennials, a layer of snow protects roots from deep ground freeze.

The Wall Microclimate

Your wall pockets store heat. Use them for plants that are slightly more frost-sensitive (like Fuchsias). Conversely, they dry out faster in summer; water these twice as often.
